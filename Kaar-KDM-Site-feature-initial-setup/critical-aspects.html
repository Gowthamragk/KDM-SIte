Critical Aspects – Governance Enablement

1. Executive Sponsorship

Strong executive sponsorship is a critical success factor for effective governance. Sponsors provide strategic direction, resolve escalations, and reinforce accountability.

2. Risk & Issue Management

Early identification and proactive mitigation of risks prevents downstream failures. Governance must enforce disciplined risk reviews and escalation mechanisms.

  • Risk identification workshops
  • Issue escalation SLAs
  • Mitigation ownership

3. Change Control Discipline

Uncontrolled changes are a major threat to project success. A formal change control process ensures scope stability and cost predictability.

4. Compliance & Audit Readiness

Governance frameworks must ensure compliance with organizational, regulatory, and contractual requirements. Audit readiness should be embedded throughout the project lifecycle.
