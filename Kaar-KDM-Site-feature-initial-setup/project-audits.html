Project Audits

Independent assessment of delivery health, governance effectiveness, and execution risks across projects and programs.

What is a Project Audit?

A project audit is a structured and independent review of a project’s execution against defined standards, methodologies, and governance frameworks. It provides leadership with objective visibility into delivery performance and risk exposure.

Why Project Audits are Critical

As projects scale in complexity and stakeholder involvement, early identification of risks becomes essential. Audits help prevent downstream failures by highlighting gaps early.

  • Early detection of delivery risks
  • Improved cost and schedule control
  • Alignment with PMO governance standards
  • Increased stakeholder confidence

Types of Audits Conducted

Depending on the lifecycle stage and business criticality, audits are conducted across multiple dimensions.

  • Initiation & Readiness Audits
  • In-flight Delivery Audits
  • Risk & Compliance Audits
  • Go-live & Closure Audits

Key Audit Focus Areas

  • Governance & reporting effectiveness
  • Scope, cost, and schedule adherence
  • Risk, issue & dependency management
  • Quality assurance & controls
  • Stakeholder communication

Audit Outcomes

Each audit results in actionable insights, prioritized recommendations, and a clear corrective action roadmap to improve delivery predictability and maturity.
