How To – Governance Enablement

1. Governance Foundation

Establishing a strong governance foundation ensures alignment across stakeholders, delivery teams, and sponsors. This includes defining governance objectives, roles, responsibilities, and decision authorities.

Clear ownership and escalation paths reduce ambiguity and ensure faster resolution of issues during execution.

2. Project Initiation Controls

Project initiation governance focuses on readiness validation before execution begins. This includes approval gates, kickoff checklists, baseline artifact validation, and stakeholder sign-offs.

  • Business case approval
  • Scope and objectives sign-off
  • Governance cadence setup

3. Execution & Monitoring

Governance during execution ensures continuous oversight of scope, cost, schedule, quality, and risks. Regular governance forums help track progress and address deviations proactively.

Metrics-driven reporting enables leadership to make informed decisions based on real-time data.

4. Closure & Continuous Improvement

Governance does not end with delivery. Closure activities include formal acceptance, lessons learned documentation, and audit closure.

Continuous improvement initiatives ensure governance maturity across future projects.
